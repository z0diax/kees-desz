FAQ

Helpful answers before the celebration

Common Questions

A few quick answers for our guests. If you still need help, you can always contact Kees or Desz directly.

What time do I need to arrive at the venue?

The entourage procession will strictly start at 3:30 PM. This will be followed by the bridal march and the wedding ceremony proper. Please arrive at least one hour ahead of the ceremony and consider the traffic and travel time.

In every wedding day, the bride should be the last person to arrive.

What time will the program start?

There will be a pre-program at 5:30 PM. Appetizers and refreshments will be served, so mingle with others and make memories with our photo booth.

The main program will start at 6:00 PM.

Can I bring a guest or date?

Unfortunately, no. We hope you understand that we can no longer accommodate those who are not on our guest list due to limited seats. Each seat has a name assigned.

Can we take photos or videos?

Please keep our ceremony camera-free. We hired an amazing team to capture the moments. Though our "I Do's" are unplugged, our reception is not. Once we finish our first kiss, you are free to take shots.

Most importantly, do not block the view of our hired professional photo and video team. We promise to share the photos after the wedding.

Can we seat anywhere during the reception?

No. We planned a seating arrangement for our guests' convenience and group familiarity. Our coordinator will gladly assist you to your designated seats.

Do you have any gift preference?

With all that we have we’ve been truly blessed, your presence and prayers are all that we request. However, if you desire to give nonetheless, we are are more than happy to receive your gift but if possible we would prefer CASH to help us save for our future. Thank you kindly!

What should we wear?

We'd love to see you dress up for our big day. Dress code is formal attire.

Ladies: Floor-length, tea-length, or midi-formal dresses.

Gentlemen: Long sleeves and slacks or trousers.

Please refer to the color palette in the invitation. Strictly no jeans, rubber shoes, slippers, and any white-colored dress, please.
